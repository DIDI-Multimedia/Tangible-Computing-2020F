Introduction

Summary

This week we will review the basics of HTML, CSS, and JS and create a personal website. We will reflect on our relationship with technology and learning. And think about the "big ideas" we want to explore this semester.

Readings

Read at least one of the following articles

  • "Computer as Material: Messing about Time" by Seymour Papert (1988)
  • "Papert's Big Idea" by Seymour Papert (2000)
  • "Ghost in the Machine: Seymour Papert on How Computers Fundamentally Change the Way Kids Learn" Interview of Seymour Papert by Dan Schwartz (1999)
  • "Mindstorms: Children, Computers, and Powerful Ideas", Chapter 2 Mathphobia: The Fear of Learning, Seymour Papert (1980)

Deliverables

  • Sign-up for a GitHub Account
  • Share your GitHub user-name with your professor immediately so they can add you to the class repository.
  • Once you have access to the class github repository, you will find a folder with your name in the 'students' folder.
  • Start your personal website. Follow these instuctions:
    • Use the template website as a starting point
    • Create a table of contents in 'index.html'. It will contain a link to a page for each of the 15 weeks of the coure.
    • Make sure to create a seperate webpage for each week. For example, week 1 would be saved in 'week_01.html'.
    • Use at least 25 different HTML tags
    • Use use at least 50 different CSS tags
    • Push to github when completed. Feel free to update as many times as you like.

  • Reflection Assignment, Week 1 Write 300-400 words reflecting on your relationship with technology. What are your goals for the course? Is there a "big idea" you hope to explore? Is there a topic you have encountered at DIDI or another school you want to learn better? What is your biggest fear with this course? Cite the readings and/or an inspiring person, project, or problem to help formulate a response. Include any sketches or images to help you explain your answer.
