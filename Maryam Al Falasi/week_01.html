In this piece I will be discussing artist and pieces I take inspiration
from, starting with Takashi Murakami, a Japanese contemporary artist,
who introduced Super-flat art and is known for mixing modernism with
traditional art, where he takes major inspiration from Andy Warhol s
works, he is also the artist behind many famous digital pieces
which include; Kanye West s 2007 Graduation album, Billie Eillish s
you should see me in a crown music video, Louis Vuitton s LV
multi-colour monogram bags, and many more... Takashi Murakami was
one of the artists which inspired me to start a career in Multimedia,
because I really loved his anime-inspired pieces.

Another Artist who I take inspiration from is Kaws, who started as a
graffiti artist, he is most commonly known for his skull like pieces
and has worked with major brands which include, Dior, Uniqlo, Kanye
West, Nike, and etc.. I found his work appealing to me because he has
his own unique touch which makes his works instantly recognizable and
I loved the way he would find any character or already existing figure
and using his artistic method would change the way the head of the
character looked like to mark his statement and create this piece of
art, I believe that his approach and style is unique and sparks
intrigue with the viewer because his art pieces are a reflection to the
undead I believe and he has such a dark take on these characters but
portrays them in a very interesting way, like his dead mickey piece
which most people are familiar with.
Art is subjective, every person views the beauty of art in their own
unique way and in my own opinion I believe that these are some of the
artists that have truly inspired me to take on this career path and
create cartoon inspired digital pieces with my own unique touch.
